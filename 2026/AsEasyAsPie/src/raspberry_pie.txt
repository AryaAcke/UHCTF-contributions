Ingredients (makes one 9" pie):

Crust (all-butter flaky crust):
- 2 1/2 cups (320 g) all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
- 1 cup (225 g) cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes
- 6–8 tbsp ice water (more if needed)

Filling:
- 5 cups fresh raspberries (about 500–600 g). If using frozen, keep them frozen until mixing.
- 3/4 cup (150 g) granulated sugar — adjust to taste depending on berry sweetness
- 1/3 cup (40 g) cornstarch (for a set filling) — or 1/2 cup (60 g) for firmer set
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
- 1/8 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp butter, cut into small bits (to dot)

Optional finishing:
- 1 egg beaten with 1 tbsp water (egg wash)
- 1–2 tbsp coarse sugar (for sprinkling on crust)

Equipment:
- 9" (23 cm) pie dish
- Rolling pin, plastic wrap, baking sheet

Steps:

Make the crust:
1. In a large bowl, whisk flour, salt, and sugar. Add cold butter cubes and cut into flour with a pastry cutter or fingertips until mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized bits of butter remaining. (Food processor pulses work too — don’t overwork.)
2. Drizzle ice water a tablespoon at a time and gently fold until dough just comes together. Dough should be shaggy but cohesive. Don’t add too much water.
3. Divide dough into two discs, wrap in plastic, and chill at least 1 hour (or up to 2 days). Chilling relaxes gluten and keeps butter cold for flakiness.

Make the filling:
4. In a bowl, gently toss raspberries with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla, and salt. Taste a berry — if very tart, add another tablespoon of sugar. Let sit 10–15 minutes so juices start forming; this helps cornstarch hydrate.

Assemble:
5. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Place a baking sheet in the oven to preheat or have one ready under the pie to catch drips.
6. Roll out one dough disc on a lightly floured surface to about 12" (30 cm) diameter. Fit into a 9" pie dish, leaving slight overhang. Chill 10 minutes if it has softened.
7. Spoon raspberry filling into crust, mounding slightly. Dot the top with small bits of butter.
8. Roll the second disc and cover with a full top crust (vent with slits) or cut into strips for a lattice. Trim excess dough, fold under bottom crust edge, and crimp to seal.
9. Brush crust with egg wash and sprinkle coarse sugar if desired.

Bake:
10. Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 20 minutes to set and start browning. Then reduce oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake 25–35 more minutes until crust is golden and filling is bubbling. Cover edges with foil if browning too fast.
11. For deeply colored filling and minimal leakage: tent loosely with foil for last 10–15 minutes, but ensure bubbling stops before removing.

Cool & serve:
12. Remove pie to a wire rack and cool at least 2–3 hours (longer is better) so filling thickens and slices cleanly. Serve slightly warm or at room temperature with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

Tips, variations & troubleshooting:
- Thickening: Cornstarch works well; for clearer glossy filling, use 2–3 tbsp tapioca starch. For very thick filling, up to 1/2 cup cornstarch.
- Flavor boosts: Add 1–2 tbsp orange or raspberry liqueur, 1 tsp almond extract, or zest of one lemon or orange.
- Frozen berries: Do not thaw fully; toss gently with sugar and cornstarch. Expect slightly longer bake.
- Crust tips: Keep all ingredients cold. Chill dough between handling. For extra flakiness, use cold butter + chilled vegetable shortening (50/50). Don’t overwork dough.
- Make-ahead: Dough can be refrigerated 2 days or frozen 2 months. Assemble and freeze unbaked pie; bake from frozen adding 10–15 minutes.
- Storage: Refrigerate covered up to 3–4 days. Freeze sliced up to 2 months; reheat gently.
- Serving: Excellent with crème fraîche or vanilla ice cream and fresh berries.
