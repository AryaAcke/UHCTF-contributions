Ingredients:

- 4 cups fresh or frozen blueberries (about 500 g)
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar (150 g)
- 3 tbsp cornstarch (or 3 tbsp flour)
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp lemon zest (optional)
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 2 tbsp butter, cut into small pieces
- 1 double pie crust (9" pan)
- 1 egg + 1 tbsp water (egg wash, optional)

Steps:

1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). If using frozen berries, don’t thaw (they hold up better).

2. In a bowl, gently stir blueberries with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, lemon zest, and salt until evenly coated. Let sit 5–10 minutes so juices start to release.

3. Fit the bottom crust into a 9" pie pan. Spoon blueberry mixture into crust and dot the top with butter pieces. Cover with top crust (cut vents or make a lattice). Trim and crimp edges. Brush with egg wash if you like a shiny crust.

4. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch drips. Bake 20 minutes at 400°F, then lower oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake 25–30 more minutes until crust is golden and filling bubbles. If the edges brown too quickly, shield with foil.

5. Remove from oven and cool on a rack for at least 1–2 hours so filling sets. Serve warm or room temperature, optionally with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

Tips:

- If you prefer a firmer filling, increase cornstarch to 4 tbsp.
- For extra flavor, add 1/2 tsp vanilla or a pinch of cinnamon.
- Store covered in the fridge up to 3 days.
